In the Castello lab, our work focuses on the biochemistry of Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV-1) - the well-known pathogen responsible for the AIDS crisis. My work focuses specifically on a crucial HIV-1 protein called Rev. Rev is a small protein that is essential for viral replication - without Rev, HIV-1 cannot replicate efficiently! Why is Rev so essential?

Rev can enter the nucleus, bind viral RNA transcripts via a hairpin region in the second intron in the transcript, and transport RNA to the cytoplasm to be translated. But what else does Rev do? We don't really know! I try to answer this question in my research:
  • Which host cell proteins does Rev interact with and what do these interactions do?
  • Where does Rev live in the cell across infection and what does this tell us about it's function?
  • Does Rev bind to host RNA as well as viral RNA? If so, which RNA and why?
